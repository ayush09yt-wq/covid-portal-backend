COVID-19 Articles

Understanding COVID-19

COVID-19 is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It spreads mainly through droplets released when an infected person talks, coughs, or sneezes. Symptoms range from mild (fever, cough, fatigue) to severe, particularly in older adults or those with pre-existing conditions. Testing and preventive measures like masks and distancing are essential to reduce transmission.

COVID-19 Vaccines

Vaccines are the cornerstone of the fight against COVID-19. They train the immune system to combat the virus, reducing severe disease, hospitalization, and death. Booster doses maintain protection against new variants. Public education and awareness campaigns are crucial to address vaccine hesitancy and misinformation.

COVID-19 Variants

The virus has evolved into multiple variants, such as Delta and Omicron, which spread more easily. While vaccines remain effective, monitoring and preventive measures are vital to limit outbreaks and protect vulnerable populations.

Mental Health During the Pandemic

COVID-19 has caused stress, anxiety, and social isolation. Coping strategies include exercise, mindfulness, virtual social interactions, and seeking professional counseling when needed.

COVID-19 in India

The first case in India was reported on 30 January 2020 in Kerala. Nationwide lockdowns and curfews were implemented to control the spread. Testing, vaccination, and containment strategies have played a key role in managing the pandemic.
